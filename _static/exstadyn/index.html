Exposing Spatio-Temporal Cellular Dynamics Within the Cells' Continuum

"A video is worth more than a thousand images."

Representing the cells' space-time continuum by linking live-cell images to paired molecular profiling data to elucidate mechanisms of cell state transitions.

From Static Inference to Dynamic Prediction

Our goal is to create a holistic representation of biological systems. Traditional methods fall short by failing to capture the continuous nature of cellular processes.

Goal

Transition from static phenotype inference to dynamic, map-based predictions of cellular systems.

Challenge

Traditional profiling methods are often destructive, losing the temporal and spatial context of cellular processes.

Contribution

An experimental-computational approach, exstadyn, that predicts cellular fate and transient paths using live-cell imaging.

The Exstadyn Methodology

A powerful pipeline combining an innovative experimental design with a sophisticated computational approach to model cellular dynamics.

The Experimental Pipeline

Live-Cell Imaging

Capturing the dynamics of cell populations over time, providing the ground truth for cell state transitions.

Static Snapshots

Coupling live imaging with destructive measurements (e.g., scRNA-seq, Cell Painting) at key time points {t1, t2... tn}.

The Computational Approach

1. Encode Static Data

Static bright-field and Cell Paint data set the basis for an enriched encoder.

2. Fine-Tune with Dynamic Data

The model is fine-tuned on time-series data, providing temporally aware representations.

3. Apply for Biological Utility

The final representations are used for specific biological tasks using dedicated modules.

The Value of Live-Cell Imaging

Live-cell imaging provides the crucial longitudinal information needed to truly understand dynamic phenotypes like resistance, proliferation, and differentiation.

What We Miss with Snapshots

  • Lacks ground truth for trajectory inference.
  • Fails to capture consecutive measurements of individual cells over time.

What We Gain with Live Imaging

  • Provides true trajectories of individual cells.
  • Enables deeper understanding of dynamic phenotypes.

A Comprehensive, Scalable Approach

We present a complete experimental-computational approach for studying cellular dynamics, designed to be scalable and adaptable across various biological conditions.

Live-Cell Imaging Ground Truth

Overcomes the lack of ground truth for model training, enabling longitudinal tracking.

Multi-Modal Applicability

Integrates information from static snapshots to enhance the representation space.

Cost and Protocol Efficiency

Reduces experimental costs and simplifies protocols compared to lineage tracing.

Unified Computational Pipeline

Offers a complete framework from pre-processing to downstream analysis tools.
