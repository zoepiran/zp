Exposing Spatio-Temporal Cellular Dynamics Within the Cells' Continuum

"A video is worth more than a thousand images."

Representing the cells' space-time continuum by linking live-cell images to paired molecular profiling data to elucidate mechanisms of cell state transitions.

From Static Inference to Dynamic Prediction

Dynamic-aware representation of biological systems, overcoming the limitations of traditional methods.

Goal

Provide a rich dynamic aware representation of biological systems.

Challenges

Traditional profiling methods are often destructive and fail to capture the cellular continuum, while non-destructive approaches suffer from lower throughput.

Contribution

An experimental-computational approach, exstadyn, that exposes spatio-temporal cellular dynamics.

Aspects of Live(-Cell Imaging)

Unlike trajectory inference from static data, live-cell imaging follows the same cells over time, providing crucial longitudinal information for dynamic phenotypes.

Ground Truth from Live Imaging

  • Captures true longitudinal tracking of individual cells; crucial for understanding dynamic phenotypes (e.g., resistance, proliferation).

Inference from Static Data

  • Provides rich morphological and molecular readouts but fails to capture consecutive measurements of individual cells over time.

The Exstadyn Methodology

An experimental-computational approach to expose spatio-temporal cellular dynamics.

Pairing Live-Imaging with Static Data

Live-Cell Imaging

Capturing the dynamics of cell populations over time.

Static Snapshots

Enriching representations with destructive measurements (e.g., scRNA-seq, Cell Painting) at key time points {t1, t2... tn}.

A Multi-Modal Model for Dynamic Cell States

1. Encode with Static Data

Static data sets the basis for an enriched multi-modal encoder.

2. Fine-Tune with Dynamic Data

The model is fine-tuned on time-series data, providing temporally aware representations.

3. Apply for Biological Utility

The final representations are used for specific biological tasks using dedicated modules.

Summary

We present a comprehensive experimental-computational approach for studying cellular dynamics, designed to be scalable and adaptable across various biological conditions.

Live-cell imaging

Overcomes the lack of ground truth for model training, enabling longitudinal tracking of cell state transitions.

Multi-modal applicability

Integrates information from static snapshots to obtain a rich representation landscape.

Cost and protocol efficiency

Reduces experimental costs and simplifies protocols compared to lineage tracing.

Unified computational pipeline

Offers data pre-processing, a learning framework to represent the spatio-temporal continuum, and diverse downstream tools for characterizing cellular states.
